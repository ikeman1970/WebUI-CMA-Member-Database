# Chapter Leadership Structure Implementation

## Overview
Implemented complete CMA chapter leadership hierarchy with detailed role descriptions, permissions, and database-level access control.

## Chapter Officer Positions (7 Total)

### 1. Chapter President
**Vision Maintainer & Servant-Leader**

**Key Responsibility**: Maintains the vision of "changing the world, one heart at a time" through servant-leadership, working with officers and members to build an effective witness for Christ.

**Core Functions**:
- Promote and attend CMA state/national events (lead by example)
- Find and get involved with local motorcycling organizations (ministry through relationships)
- Communicate with chapter members (listen actively)
- Communicate with state leadership (relay national information promptly)
- Work issues through Area Rep → State Coordinator → National Evangelist chain
- Attend chapter meetings with prepared agenda
- Be committed to resolving chapter issues
- Maintain calendar for scheduling activities
- Hold monthly officer's meetings
- Be aware of other officers' job functions
- Keep chapter informed about ministry activities
- Promote Member Training course
- Ensure chapter members know and follow CMA logo policies

**Chain of Command**: Area Rep → State Coordinator → National Evangelist → VP Evangelistic Outreach

**Leadership Principle**: When things go well, praise members and give glory to God. When things don't, accept the blame. This earns respect and motivates members to work harder and step out of their comfort zone.

**Database Access**: Admin (can manage chapter data, approve expenditures, access all chapter records)

---

### 2. Chapter Vice President
**Presidential Assistant & Support**

**Key Responsibility**: Assistant to President, doing everything needed in chapter, filling in for other officers when they're absent.

**Core Functions**:
- Communicate and work as team with Chapter President
- Be available to assist other officers and fill in as needed
- Attend chapter and officer's meetings
- Welcome visitors and members at meetings
- Be familiar with CMA guidelines and procedures for helping new/prospective members
- Promote ministry
- Encourage chapter members

**Chain of Command**: Chapter President → Area Rep → State Coordinator → National Evangelist → VP Evangelistic Outreach

**Leadership Principle**: You are a servant to the chapter. As a servant, you have great influence—determine if it will be positive or negative.

**Database Access**: Officer (can view chapter data, see member information)

---

### 3. Chapter Secretary
**Record Keeper & Administrator**

**Key Responsibility**: Note taker and record keeper for the chapter. Must be dependable—decisions may be based on your records. Your presence at meetings is critical.

**Core Functions**:
- Attend chapter and officer's meetings
- Record legible, accurate minutes of chapter meetings (type and store electronically + binder)
- Have past minutes available at meetings for reference
- Keep attendance records (sign-in sheets) at chapter and secular meetings/events
- Track voting and nomination eligibility based on attendance
- Electronically fill out Chapter Quarterly Report (with President's approval)

**Quarterly Reports Due**:
- 1st Quarter: April 10
- 2nd Quarter: July 10
- 3rd Quarter: October 10
- 4th Quarter: January 10

**Additional Responsibilities**:
- Compile data from monthly sign-in sheets and Member Meeting Summary Reports
- Submit New Chapter Officers form after annual elections
- Submit changes in chapter officers via online form
- Maintain Member Information Sheets for chapter use

**Chain of Command**: Chapter President → Area Rep → State Coordinator → National Evangelist → VP Evangelistic Outreach

**Leadership Principle**: Organization is critical from the beginning. Keep detailed, accurate records. Discuss concerns with President and work as a team.

**Database Access**: Admin (can manage chapter data, track attendance, submit reports, access all records)

---

### 4. Chapter Treasurer
**Financial Manager & Steward**

**Key Responsibility**: Collects and maintains donations/finances for the chapter. Leads by example in accountability and humility.

**Core Functions**:
- Meet with officers to determine donation amounts and frequency to CMA National
- Help chapter understand importance of supporting local chapter AND national organization
- Collect donations, keeping three categories separate:
  - Chapter donations
  - Run for the Son (RFS) donations
  - CMA National donations

**Donation Guidelines**:
- Checks for chapter: Made out to chapter (NOT tax-deductible)
- Checks for CMA National/RFS: Made out to CMA National with member number and RFS designation (TAX-DEDUCTIBLE)
- Donation checks CANNOT be split—require separate checks
- Cash for CMA National: Deposit in chapter account, then write chapter check with member name/number

**Financial Responsibilities**:
- Maintain accurate records of all monies received/dispersed
- Keep itemized receipts for deposits and expenditures
- Keep financial records separate by purpose (postage, newsletter, Christmas, etc.)
- Get President's approval for ALL expenditures
- Report monthly expenditures and account status at chapter meetings
- Count donations with another officer (not spouse) before leaving meeting
- Present bank statements for review at officer meetings
- Mail CMA National donations directly to CMA National (do NOT deposit in chapter account)

**Chapter Checking Account Setup**:
- Obtain EIN from IRS (SS-4 form at irs.gov)
- Select "Community or Volunteer Group" category
- Use chapter name with chapter number
- NOTE: Chapters are NOT non-profit—member donations to chapter are NOT tax-deductible

**Chain of Command**: Chapter President → Area Rep → State Coordinator → National Evangelist → VP Evangelistic Outreach

**Leadership Principle**: Lead by example in accountability and humility. People follow leaders who are already doing the task.

**Database Access**: Officer with financial responsibilities (can view chapter data, access financial records, track donations)

---

### 5. Chapter Road Captain
**Ride Planner & Safety Leader**

**Key Responsibility**: Determines routes and leads rides. Ensures everyone has safe, enjoyable riding experience.

**Core Functions**:
- Plan rides in advance (tentatively schedule 6 months out, present at officer's meetings for approval)
- Announce ride information clearly (dates, times, locations, distance, costs)
- Record ride attendance (if Secretary absent)
- Seek new ideas and routes (varies ride types: map runs, scavenger hunts, etc.)
- Store ride ideas/items for future reference
- Appoint rear-rider for communication and support on larger rides
- Check route and current road conditions before ride
- Practice and promote safe riding habits
- Write monthly newsletter article (ride info + safety tips)
- Consider scheduling group ride instruction classes (new/interested riders)
- Attend chapter and officer's meetings

**Safety Resources**:
- Motorcycle Safety Foundation: www.msf-usa.org
- Group Ride Guidelines: http://www.msf-usa.org/downloads/Group_Ride.pdf

**Key Leadership Principle**: Before planning or leaving on a ride, pray. If God is not in our plans, we waste our time.

**Database Access**: Officer (can view chapter data, see member information for ride planning)

---

### 6. Chapter Chaplain
**Spiritual Leader & Encourager**

**Key Responsibility**: Delivers devotions and spiritual encouragement. Leads chapter into being "chaplains" to the secular world.

**Core Functions**:

**Spiritual Leadership**:
- Be a prayer warrior (prayer is foundation of chapters)
- Assemble prayer teams for divine appointments at secular events
- Pray for chapter needs with power and Holy Spirit discernment
- Keep prayers simple and reflect God's love

**Encouragement & Teaching**:
- Be an encourager to chapter members
- Deliver short nondenominational devotions (under 10 minutes, encouraging and challenging)
- Establish and lead Bible study (study, fellowship, prayer groups)
- Take prayer requests at chapter meetings
- Write uplifting, challenging monthly newsletter article
- Visit sick members; encourage and pray for them
- Accompany President on member visits (pastoral care)

**Duties**:
- Attend chapter and officer's meetings

**Key Leadership Principle**: God's love drew us to Him. Allowing God's love to flow through you will draw people closer to Christ.

**Database Access**: Officer with spiritual focus (can view chapter data, access member information for pastoral care, see emergency contacts)

---

### 7. Chapter RFS Lead
**Run for the Son Fundraising Coordinator**

**Key Responsibility**: Leads Run for the Son fundraising and coordination efforts.

**Core Functions**:
- Coordinate chapter participation in Run for the Son
- Manage RFS donations and records
- Communicate RFS goals and progress to chapter
- Coordinate with state RFS Lead
- Work with treasurer on donation tracking

**Chain of Command**: Chapter President → State RFS Lead → National

**Database Access**: Officer with fundraising responsibilities (can view chapter data, access RFS donation records)

| **EmergencyContact** | emergencycontact_chapter_officer_access | SELECT | Emergency contacts of chapter members |

## Key Design Principles

1. **Servant Leadership**: All roles emphasize serving chapter members and community
2. **Transparency**: Officers have visibility into their chapter's full operations
3. **Accountability**: Admin roles (President, Secretary) manage records and reports
4. **Specialization**: Officers have specific domains (finance, spirituality, rides, etc.)
5. **Communication**: Chain of command ensures information flows properly
6. **Community Focus**: Emphasis on relationships with local motorcyclists and secular community
